SIMULATED — educational debrief

What just happened

You interacted with a controlled phishing simulation built for the Network Security mini-project. The form you saw was a fake login page served by the local development server. No real email was sent, no real organization was impersonated, and nothing you typed was read, transmitted, or stored. The only thing recorded was that the form was submitted — not what was in it.

Why this matters

Most successful real-world attacks follow the same pattern this simulation reproduces: a plausible message, a credible-looking link, and a login form that mimics a service you trust. The most effective defense is to pause before clicking, verify the destination, and never enter credentials on a page reached through a link in an unexpected message.
